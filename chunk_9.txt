Strikingly, all BDPErucamide was taken up by CD11b+ cells, providing additional evidence that CD11b+ cells are the direct target of erucamide in the retinas (Fig. 3d). As observed in rd10 mice, CD11b+ cells in WT C57BL/6J retinas were activated across the retinal vascular layers by erucamide; however, unlike rd10 retinas, erucamide-induced CD11b+ cell accumulation in the ONL was not sig- nificant in WT eyes (Fig. 3e,f). Also, there was no aggressive choroidal neovascularization in both rd10 and WT mice after erucamide intra- vitreal injections (Extended Data Fig. 1d–f). To further explore the potential effects of erucamide on the target cells, we isolated CD11b+ cells by flow cytometry and evaluated the expression of pro-angiogenic genes within the cells by quantitative PCR (qPCR). We found that the expression of PDGFα, CTGF, PDGFβ, CX3CL1, Ang1 and BDNF was higher in CD11b+ cells of erucamide-treated ani- mals (Fig. 3g). Furthermore, the findings translated to human tissue.
