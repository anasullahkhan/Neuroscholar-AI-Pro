Levels of mRNA expres- sion were normalized to those in controls as determined using the comparative CT (ΔΔCT) method. RNA samples were used for RNA-seq in Scripps Genetic Cores. Quality control of raw sequencing data was performed using FastQC and MultiQC56. Raw reads were aligned to the reference genome using RSubread57. Gene expression levels were quantified using featureCounts. Post-alignment quality control included assessing the distribution of reads across genomic features (for example, exons, introns) and checking for potential batch effects using principal component analysis. Differential expression analysis was carried out using the limma-voom pipeline1. Data were filtered and normalization was performed using the trimmed mean of M-values method. Linear modeling and empirical Bayes moderation were applied to identify differentially expressed genes. Genes with a false discovery rate-adjusted P value < 0.05 were considered significantly differen- tially expressed. Overrepresentation analysis and functional enrich- ment analysis were performed with the clusterProfiler58 package using Gene Ontology and KEGG to define biologically relevant pathways and enrichment analysis was conducted. Cryosection and immunohistochemistry For cryosections, eyecups were fixed in 4% paraformaldehyde (PFA) at 4 °C for 4 h. Under a dissecting microscope, a cut was carefully made around the cornea, and the eyecups were transferred to 20% sucrose in PBS at 4 °C for overnight. Samples were embedded in OCT compound (Tissue-Tek, 4566) with the eyecup oriented dorsal side up and the optic nerve positioned at the bottom of the cryomold, then snap-frozen in chilled isopentane on dry ice. Once frozen solid, blocks were wrapped and stored at –80 °C until sectioning. For cryosections, blocks were equilibrated in the cryostat chamber (–20 °C) for 1 h, mounted dorsal side down (optic nerve at the bottom), and sectioned at a thickness of 10 µm. Sections were air-dried and processed for immunohistochemistry using standard protocols59. Briefly, slides were permeabilized and blocked in 2% BSA in PBS containing 0.1% Triton X-100 at room temperature for 1 h, followed by incubation with primary antibodies at 4 °C overnight. The following primary antibodies were used: Recoverin (rabbit; Millipore, AB5585; 1:500 dilution), isolectin GS-IB4 (Griffonia simplicifolia, Alexa Fluor 568 conjugated; Thermo Fisher Scientific, I21412; 1:1,000 dilution). Secondary antibodies were obtained from Life Technologies, and the DAB staining kit was purchased from Vector Laboratories. Retinal whole-mount staining Retinal whole mounts were prepared as previously described with minor modifications52. Eyecups were fixed in 4% PFA at 4 °C for 1 h, then transferred to individual wells of a 48-well plate containing PBS. The cornea was carefully removed using fine forceps and scissors, followed by gentle separation of the sclera and choroid from the retina, avoiding direct contact with the retinal tissue to prevent damage. The lens and hyaloid vasculature were removed, and the isolated retina was used for immunohistochemistry. Samples were incubated with primary antibodies overnight at 4 °C.
