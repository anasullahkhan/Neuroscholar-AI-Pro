The instrument was set to acquire over the m/z range of 25–1,500, an acquisition rate of 1.3 spectra per second. MS/MS was performed in auto mode, and the instrument was set to acquire over the m/z range of 25–1,500, an acquisition rate of 2 spectra per second for MS, 1.5 spectra per second for MS/MS with an iso width (the width half-maximum of the quadrupole mass bandpass used during MS/MS precursor isola- tion) of 1.3 m/z. MS/MS scans a maximum of five precursor ions at the same time. It stops after three scans for the same ions, then released after 0.4 min. The cycle time is 3.9 s. For erucamide detection in rd10 mice and human peripheral retina, analyses were performed on a Waters I-Class LC and a Waters SQD2 single-quadrupole MS operated in positive ESI mode. A Waters Acquity UPLC BEH C18 column (1.7 μm, 2.1 × 50 mm) with an 85–99% B gradient lasting 2.4 min (A: 0.1% aque- ous formic acid, B: 0.1% formic acid in methanol, 0.6 ml min−1 flow rate, 55 °C column temperature) was used. The product identity and rela- tive quantification were determined by mass spectrometry (Waters Unispray, positive SIRs). LC–MS data processing and statistics LC–MS data from the retina extract were processed using the bioinfor- matics analysis software XCMS Online (https://xcmsonline.scripps. edu/). The experimental MS/MS spectral data were compared with the reference erucamide MS/MS spectra from the METLIN database (both at a collision energy of 20 eV)17,18,53,54. MS/MS spectra were manually processed by extracting ion chromatograms using Qualitative Analysis --- Page 15 --- Nature Neuroscience Article https://doi.org/10.1038/s41593-026-02341-w of MassHunter Workstation (Agilent Technologies). pNLC and BPC extractions were performed using MassHunter. Preparation of pSi Freestanding pSi flakes were purchased from Trutag, prepared using a previously developed ‘perforated etch’ method55. The pSiNPs were obtained by immersing the pSi flakes in ethanol in a sealed vial, which was then immersed in an ultrasonication bath (Ultrasonic bath, 97043-960, VWR International) and subjected to ultrasonic fracture conditions for 24 h. The nanoparticle suspension was centrifuged at 1,500g for 20 min to remove large particles at the bottom of the tubes. The nanoparticle suspensions were then centrifuged at high speed (10,000g, 20 min) to pelletize the nanoparticles. The superna- tant was discarded to eliminate the smallest nanoparticle fragments that remained in the suspension, and the pellet was resuspended in fresh ethanol. The high-speed centrifugation/resuspension proce- dure was carried out two more times to thoroughly wash the nano- particles. The resulting pSiNPs displayed an average hydrodynamic size of ~170 nm. Surface modification of pSiNPs The collected pSiNPs were washed and centrifuged (15,000g, 15 min) twice with toluene to remove residual ethanol. The pSiNPs were then resuspended in toluene and transferred into a glass vial, and n-octadecylsilane was added.
