The needle was maintained in position for an additional 10 s to minimize reflux, then withdrawn slowly over another 10 s (ref. 52). Success injections were verified by the absence of leakage and maintenance of ocular integrity under a dissecting microscope. Both male and female mice were included in all experimental groups, and no sex-dependent differences were observed in their responses to Eru treatment. For C18-pSi and Eru-C18-pSi injections in rd10, WT and RhoP23H mice, bilateral injections were performed. Within the same littermate, pups were randomized to control (C18-pSi) or treatment (Eru-C18-pSi) groups. For BDPErucamide injection, bilateral injections were performed for C18-pSi and BDPEru-C18-pSi groups as the original Eru-C18-pSi design. For in vivo knockdown experiments, all littermate mice received intravitreal injections of AAV2 viruses. The left eye was injected with an AAV2 carrying a scrambled shRNA control (AAV2-Scr, 2.02 × 1012 genome copies (GCs) per ml), while the right eye received an AAV2 expressing shRNA targeting TMEM19 (AAV2-shTMEM19, 2.14 × 1012 GCs per ml). Five days after the injection, mice were randomly assigned to two treatment groups and bilaterally injected with either C18-pSi or Eru-C18-pSi nanoparticles. The shRNA sequences used were as follows: Scr-shRNA, 5′-CAACAAGATGAAGAGCACC-3′; shT- MEM19, 5′-ACGTGCAAAAGGTATATAAAG-3′. AAV2 vectors and other custom reagents are available from the corresponding author upon reasonable request. For in vivo neutralizing antibody inhibition experiments, the fol- lowing antibodies were bilaterally co-injected with either C18-pSi or Eru-C18-pSi in rd10 mice (vendor, catalog number and concentration indicated): TNF (R&D systems, MAB410, 250 ng μl−1), Rat IgG1 Isotype Control (control for TNF, R&D systems, MAB005, 250 ng μl−1), FGF-2 (05-117, Sigma-Aldrich, 1 μg μl−1), Mouse IgG1 Isotype Control (con- trol for FGF, R&D systems, MAB002, 1 μg μl−1), VEGF164 (R&D systems, AF-493-NA, 200 ng μl−1) and Goat IgG Control (control for VEGF164, R&D systems, AB-108-C, 200 ng μl−1). Ocular metabolite extraction and sample storage Metabolite extractions were performed using either whole eyes (for RCS and WT rats) or isolated retinas (for rd10 and WT mice, and human donor samples), as specified below. For RCS and WT rat samples, rodents were euthanized, and whole eyes were immediately enucle- ated, rinsed in ice-cold PBS, snap-frozen on dry ice, and stored at −80 °C until extraction. For rd10 and WT mouse samples, eyes were enucleated and placed in PBS on ice. Under a dissection microscope, the retina was carefully separated from the rest of the eyeball and immediately processed for extraction. For human donor samples, peripheral retina from postmortem eyes was dissected and processed similarly. Tissue samples were transferred into 2-ml glass homogeniz- ers (Wheaton) and homogenized in a cold extraction solvent consist- ing of methanol–chloroform–water–acetic acid (60:25:14:1, vol/vol), a fully miscible mixture optimized for broad-spectrum metabolite recovery.
