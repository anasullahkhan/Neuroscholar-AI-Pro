Thermogravimetric analysis measurements indicated that the particles were composed of approximately 6% (wt%) erucamide, based on the mass change upon oxidative combustion at 950 °C (Extended Data Fig. 2f,g). Erucamide delivery via pSiNPs rescues retinal degeneration Next, we investigated the potential therapeutic function of erucamide in the mammalian retina. rd10 mice were used in these experiments as vaso-degeneration and photoreceptor degeneration start at P16–P20 and occur in a stereotypical temporal sequence29, and erucamide levels are spontaneously attenuated by P12 (Extended Data Fig. 1a). We intra- vitreally injected 214 ng of erucamide into the vitreous of rd10 at P12 and P22 through Eru-C18-pSi (Fig. 2a). We observed that the extent of photoreceptor atrophy was significantly reduced in rd10 mice at P32 with erucamide. The thicknesses of both the outer nuclear layer (ONL)30 and the inner nuclear layer (INL) were rescued by erucamide. (Fig. 2b,c). To assess retinal function, we performed electroretinography (ERG) recordings across a range of stimulus intensities. Erucamide-treated rd10 mice exhibited increased scotopic B-wave amplitudes compared with vehicle controls across intensities, reaching statistical significance at higher flash strengths (50 cd s/m2; Fig. 2d,e). These results indicate a rod-dominated, intensity-dependent functional rescue in a geneti- cally degenerating retina. Additionally, erucamide significantly pro- tected the intraretinal deep plexus vasculature from vaso-obliteration compared to vehicle-injected controls (Fig. 2f,g).
