Cell culture The HMC3 cell line (CRL-3304) was purchased from the American Type Culture Collection (ATCC) and cultured with Eagle’s Minimum Essential Medium (EMEM, 30-2003, ATCC). The HEK293T cell line (CRL-3216) was purchased from the ATCC and cultured with Dulbecco’s Modi- fied Eagle’s Medium (DMEM, 30-2002, ATCC). For transfection with mTMEM19-Flag plasmid, HEK293T cells were seeded at 1 × 104 cells per cm2 in 2 ml complete medium and grown for 24 h until 80% confluent. Transfection complexes of plasmid (Mouse TMEM19 Gene ORF cDNA clone expression plasmid, C-Flag tag, SinoBiological, MG55126-NF) and Lipofectamine 2000 (Invitrogen, 11668030) were formed at room temperature in serum-free medium before drop-wise addition to the cells, followed by incubation for 5 h and replacement with complete medium for 48 h. IPS cell MPCs were derived from peripheral blood mononuclear cells from a female donor with informed consent. The cell reprogramming was performed by the Harvard iPS core facility utilizing a Sendai virus for reprogramming factor delivery. iPS cells were maintained on Cultrex (R&D systems)-coated plates immersed with mTesR+ medium (STEMCELL Technologies). The cells were pas- saged every 3 days around 80% confluence. Colonies containing clearly visible differentiated cells were marked and manually removed before any subsequent passaging. All MPCs were generated using previously described methods34,35. Primary human umbilical vein endothelial cells (PCS-100-010) were purchased from the ATCC and cultured with Vas- cular Cell Basal Medium (PCS-100-030) with Endothelial Cell Growth Kit-VEGF (PCS-100-041). Human choroidal endothelial cells (36052-03) were purchased from Celprogen and cultured with Human Choroidal Endothelial Cells Complete Growth Media with Serum (M36052-03S). Human retinal endothelial primary cells (36205-33) were purchased from Celprogen and cultured with Human Retinal Endothelial Cell Culture Media with Serum (M36205-33S). All cells were obtained with verified normal karyotype and were free from contamination. PAL method HMC3 cells and HEK293T cells were treated with vehicle (ethanol) or a lipid probe for 3 h in the incubator. HMC3 cells were then UV irradiated (10 min) and lysed, and lipid probe-labeled proteins were conjugated to biotin azide by CuAAC reaction. The biotin-labeled proteins were then enriched with streptavidin beads and trypsin digested, and the digested peptides were labeled with TMT, followed by combination, HPLC fractionation and LC–MS/MS analysis36. HEK293T cells were then UV irradiated then UV irradiated and lysed, and lipid probe-labeled pro- teins were conjugated to Rhodamine by CuAAC reaction.
