Under a dissection microscope, the retina was carefully separated from the rest of the eyeball and immediately processed for extraction. For human donor samples, peripheral retina from postmortem eyes was dissected and processed similarly. Tissue samples were transferred into 2-ml glass homogeniz- ers (Wheaton) and homogenized in a cold extraction solvent consist- ing of methanol–chloroform–water–acetic acid (60:25:14:1, vol/vol), a fully miscible mixture optimized for broad-spectrum metabolite recovery. The initial homogenization lasted 30 s, followed by the addition of 200 μl chilled extraction solvent (4 °C) and further homog- enization for 90 s. This was followed by two additional homogeniza- tion cycles using 600 μl and 400 μl extraction solvent, respectively. After each cycle, the homogenizer was rinsed thoroughly to minimize sample loss. The combined homogenate (~1,200 μl total, including eye fluids) was transferred to a pre-chilled microcentrifuge tube and snap-frozen in liquid nitrogen for 1 min. All homogenates were kept on dry ice during processing. After thawing on ice (4 °C), samples were centrifuged at 16,000g for 10–15 min at 4 °C. The supernatant was collected and optionally stored overnight at −20 °C to allow fur- ther precipitation. Samples were then dried using a SpeedVac con- centrator without heating. The dried extracts were reconstituted in acetonitrile–water (1:0.8, vol/vol) containing 5% methanol, 5% chlo- roform and 0.1% formic acid. Samples were vortexed vigorously or sonicated for 1 min, incubated at 4 °C for ~1 h and centrifuged again to remove debris. Supernatants were transferred to LC–MS vials for metabolomic analysis. LC–MS For untargeted metabolites in RCS and WT rats, analyses were per- formed using a 1260 HPLC system (Agilent Technologies) coupled to a 6538 UHD Accurate-Mass Q-TOF (Agilent Technologies) operated in positive (ESI+) and negative (ESI−) ionization modes. Standards or eye extracts were separated using a Waters Xbridge C18, 3.5 mm, 135 A, 150 mm × 1.0 mm; that is, when the instrument was operated in positive ionization mode, the solvent system was A = 0.1% formic acid in water, and B = 0.1% formic acid in acetonitrile. The gradient elution starts with 100% A for injection at 0 min, increases to 80% A at 5 min and reaches 0% A at 43 min, then decreases B from 100% to 98% from 43 min to 55 min, then drops to 5% at 60 min. The injection volume was 4 μl. ESI conditions included: gas temperature, 325 °C; drying gas, 11 l min−1; nebulizer, 30 psig; fragmentor, 124 V; and skimmer, 65 V. The instrument was set to acquire over the m/z range of 25–1,500, an acquisition rate of 1.3 spectra per second. MS/MS was performed in auto mode, and the instrument was set to acquire over the m/z range of 25–1,500, an acquisition rate of 2 spectra per second for MS, 1.5 spectra per second for MS/MS with an iso width (the width half-maximum of the quadrupole mass bandpass used during MS/MS precursor isola- tion) of 1.3 m/z.
