We confirmed the interaction between erucamide and TMEM19 by overexpressing TMEM19 in HEK293T cells and observed that overexpressed TMEM19 is prominently labeled by erucamide probes compared to control probes (Fig. 4d). These data indicate that TMEM19 is a receptor for erucamide and may mediate erucamide’s effect on cell function. TMEM19-dependent downstream signaling mediates neuroprotection TMEM19 is predicted to have six transmembrane domains; however, its function remains unknown (Fig. 5a). We further investigated the effect of erucamide on myeloid cells through TMEM19 by analyzing bulk RNA sequencing (RNA-seq) in iPS cell-MPCs treated with eru- camide with TMEM19 knocked down. TMEM19 was knocked down in iPS cell-MPCs using siTMEM19 or scrambled short interfering RNA (Scr siRNA) as control, then cells were treated with either empty nanoparticles (C18-pSi) or erucamide (Eru-C18-pSi). Differential expression analysis was performed to compare the treatment combi- nations. We first performed the comparisons between Scr cells with and without erucamide (Extended Data Fig. 5a) and siTMEM19 cells separately (Extended Data Fig. 5c); subsequently, we compared dif- ferences from the two comparisons against each other in Fig. 5b.
