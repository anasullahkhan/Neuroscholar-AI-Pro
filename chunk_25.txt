RCS rats were obtained from M. LaVail, UCSF. rd10 mice (B6. CXB1-Pde6brd10/J), WT mice (C57BL/6J) and RhoP23H mice (B6.129S6(Cg)-Rhotm1.1Kpal/J) were obtained from the Jackson Labs. All animals were housed under a 12-h light–12-h dark cycle at an ambi- ent temperature of 22 ± 2 °C and 45–65% relative humidity, with ad libitum access to standard rodent chow and water. All intravitreal injections were performed under anesthesia to ensure animal welfare and procedural precision. Mice were anesthetized via intraperitoneal injection of ketamine (100 mg per kg body weight) and xylazine (10 mg per kg body weight). For neonatal mice (<P14) with unopened eyes, the eyelids were carefully separated using blunt forceps to create a small fissure along the natural eyelid margin. To establish an entry site, a 0.5-ml 31-gauge insulin syringe (Sol-Millennium, 1653151B-PK) was first inserted and withdrawn at the limbus, followed by injection using a 5-μl Hamilton syringe fitted with a 33-gauge, 0.5-inch sharp needle (point style 4; Hamilton, 7803-15). The needle was advanced toward the center of the vitreous cavity while avoiding contact with the lens. A total of 0.5 μl of solution was injected into the vitreous cav- ity for mice younger than P16, and 1 μl for mice older than P16, using minimal, constant pressure over 10 s. The needle was maintained in position for an additional 10 s to minimize reflux, then withdrawn slowly over another 10 s (ref. 52). Success injections were verified by the absence of leakage and maintenance of ocular integrity under a dissecting microscope.
